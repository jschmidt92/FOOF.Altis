# Contributing to Forward Offensive

Forward Offensive, also known as FOOF, is a proprietary Frontline Operations
Development Group project. Contributions are welcome only when they can be used
inside FOOF under the project's license and release model.

## Contribution Terms

By submitting code, configs, UI, documentation, assets, bug fixes, design notes,
or other project material to this repository, you confirm that:

- You have the right to submit the contribution.
- Your contribution is your own work, or you have permission to contribute it.
- Your contribution does not include code, assets, or content copied from another
  project unless that source is clearly identified and compatible with FOOF.
- You grant Frontline Operations Development Group a perpetual, worldwide,
  royalty-free right to use, modify, publish, package, distribute, and sublicense
  your contribution as part of Forward Offensive.
- You understand that Forward Offensive is not open source, and your contribution
  may be included in proprietary public or private FOOF releases.

If you do not agree to these terms, do not submit the contribution.

## Third-Party and Pre-Existing Work

Do not submit third-party code or assets without clearly identifying the source,
original author, and license or permission terms.

IDS Logistics was originally made by jschmidt92 and has been adapted for FOOF.
Any future work touching IDS Logistics should preserve attribution and avoid
removing author history or source notes.

If a contribution modifies pre-existing third-party work, explain what changed
and keep the original attribution intact.

## Pull Requests

Keep pull requests focused and easy to review.

- Use concise titles that name the affected system.
- Explain gameplay or server behavior changes clearly.
- Call out multiplayer locality, persistence, and JIP risks.
- Include testing notes when the change affects gameplay behavior.
- Avoid unrelated formatting churn or broad refactors unless requested.

## License Reminder

Submitting a contribution does not make FOOF open source. The repository license
still controls reuse, redistribution, repacking, reuploading, hosting, modified
versions, and derivative projects.
